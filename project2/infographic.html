Water Content in Body Organs

Brain

Brain: 73% water

The brain controls and coordinates actions and reactions, allows us to think and feel, and enables us to have memories and feelings.

Lungs

Lungs: 83% water

The lungs are responsible for the exchange of oxygen and carbon dioxide between the air we breathe and our blood.

Stomach

Stomach: 75% water

The stomach breaks down food into a usable form, extracting necessary nutrients to provide energy and building blocks for the body.

Heart

Heart: 73% water

The heart pumps blood throughout the body, supplying oxygen and nutrients to tissues and removing carbon dioxide and other wastes.

Liver

Liver: 71% water

The liver processes nutrients absorbed from the gut, detoxifies harmful substances, and produces proteins and biochemicals necessary for digestion and growth.

Kidneys

Kidneys: 79% water

The kidneys filter waste products from the blood, regulate blood pressure, and maintain the balance of electrolytes and other substances in the body.

Joints

Joints: 80% water

Joints connect bones, allow movement and provide mechanical support. They are essential for locomotion and a range of bodily movements.
